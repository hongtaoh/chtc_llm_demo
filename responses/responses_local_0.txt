Bias refers to the error introduced by the model's underlying assumptions or parameters, which can lead to poor predictions when the data distribution deviates from these assumptions. Bias is typically measured using statistical measures such as mean squared error (MSE) for regression problems and root mean square error (RMSE) for classification tasks.

Variance, on the other hand, represents how much the model's prediction errors vary across different instances of the same dataset. Higher variance indicates more variability in the model's predictions, while lower variance suggests less variation.

In machine learning, the goal is to minimize both bias and variance. The objective function often involves selecting hyperparameters that balance these factors. Techniques like cross-validation help in balancing bias and variance by providing a range of possible models with varying levels of bias and variance.
